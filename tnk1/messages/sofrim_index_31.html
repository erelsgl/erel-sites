שוטר

מאת: אבנר רמו


The association: שפטים ושטרים - “Judges and officers” appears in several biblical verses (Deu 16:18; Jos 8:33; 23:2; 24:1; 1 Ch 23:4; 26:29). Yet for unknown reason the Greek translators, instead of: “officers” wrote: “recorders” or “scribes.”

In the Book of Chronicles we also read: ומהלוים, סופרים ושטרים ושוערים - “and of the Levites there were scribes, and officers, and porters” (2 Ch 34:13; see also: 2 Ch 26:11).

Here the Greek translator wrote: “scribes and Judges” which suggests that he knew that the word סופרים means “scribes.” Therefore he also recognized that here שטרים (shotrim) could not mean “scribes” and therefore “changed” it to “judges” as if שטרים (shotrim) is a misspelled שפטים (shophtim).

Although it was suggested that the Hebrew שוטר (shoter) - “officer” is related to the Akkadian word: “sataru” - “writing”, the job description of the biblical שוטר (shoter) is of a caretaker of the ruler’s instructions, and as of a guardian of public order and safety (Ex 5:6, 10, 14, 15, 19; Num 11:16; Deu 1:15, 16:18; 20:5, 8, 9; 31:28; Jos 1:10; 3:2; 8:33; 23:2; 24:1; 1 Ch 23:4; 26:29; 27:1; 2 Ch 19:11). We may therefore wonder whether this Hebrew word is related to Greek word for the spirit of safety: Σωτηρ (soter).
